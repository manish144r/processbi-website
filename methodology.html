How We Work

A disciplined, process-first business process consulting methodology refined across 15+ years and 50+ enterprise projects in Australian government, financial services, and utilities. Our structured engagement model covers everything from process baseline workshops and business process audit through Lean Six Sigma redesign, process automation consulting, and Microsoft Fabric implementation in Australia. Every engagement starts with understanding how the business works — before a single technology decision is made. No surprises, no scope creep, and no automation of broken processes.

00
Pre-engagement (1–3 days)

Phase 0 — Process Baseline & Business Process Audit

Before any architecture decisions are made, we run a structured business process audit — mapping the current process landscape using process mapping workshops in Australia (swim lane sessions, SIPOC analysis, value stream mapping) and documenting the AS-IS state. This establishes the process baseline that all subsequent technology decisions are anchored to. For organisations with complex cross-functional processes — or those operating under APRA, AML/CTF, or ASIC obligations — this phase routinely surfaces the root causes that technology was never going to fix, and the compliance gaps that hadn't been formally documented. What organisations in Australia typically discover at this stage: manual workarounds that have become de-facto process, compliance obligations not reflected in current workflows, and automation opportunities that aren't visible until the process is drawn end-to-end.

01
Week 1–2

Discovery & Audit

Deep dive into your current processes, data architecture, tooling, costs, and pain points. We audit your Power BI or Microsoft Fabric tenancy, identify capacity waste, map data flows, and quantify the cost of the status quo — in terms a CFO can act on. For process-heavy engagements, this phase includes business process mapping workshops to document current-state workflows, SIPOC analysis to bound process scope, and a prioritised automation candidate register. For data platform engagements, we baseline query performance, model complexity, refresh reliability, and governance maturity. Output: a ranked findings report with a concrete recommended path forward, a realistic business case, and — for Australian enterprise — an explicit mapping to relevant regulatory obligations (APRA, AML/CTF, ASIC, DTA).
